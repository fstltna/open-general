Some units, the numbers are unknown and vary from 3,000 to 40,000, reached Wenck's front on the morning of May 1st and were immediately send to the Elbe.
In any case, considering the overwhelming odds against them, the unification of the 9th and 12th Armies was a considerable feat for which both generals deserve full credit.
At midnight on May 7th, the crossing of the Elbe was completed and some 100,000 soldiers and 300,000 civilians had been successfully evacuated.

Presumably about 60,000 troops and refugees remained trapped in the pocket and got killed. The cemetery at Halbe today comprises about 22,000 graves meaning that many of the thousands who were killed are still buried in the soils of the forests...
