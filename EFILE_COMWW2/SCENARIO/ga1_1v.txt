Historical Note: Malta was one of the most heavily bombed areas of the entire war. The intensity with which Hitler pounded Malta attested to the importance of the island to the Mediterranean theatre of war, but in the spring of 1942, when Spitfires and Hurricanes flown from the deck of the carrier HMS Eagle arrived at the battered island, the battle over Malta took a new turn. At last, though still heavily outnumbered, the volunteer pilots from Britain, Australia, America, Canada, and New Zealand were able to put up a meaningful defense. Never again would the Axis raids be met only with token resistance, and gradually the Allied fighters began to dominate the sky above the beleaguered island. They had arrived in the nick of time...
