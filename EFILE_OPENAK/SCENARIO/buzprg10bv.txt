Combat operations in Lesser Fatra were not only bloody, but also extremely demanding when it comes to soldiers' physical and mental powers. Soldiers were fighting in altitudes reaching 1000 metres above the sea level, in a harsh weather and against determined enemy. The success of Mala Fatra operation says much about Czechoslovaks' capabilities.
