Battle of Dukla Pass went on for several weeks and it was the most cruel battle of the 1st Czechoslovak Army Corps since its very beginning. On the 9th September 1944 a miscommunication and poor reconnaisance lead the 1st and 3rd brigades right into the enemy positions. Germans, having in place even the dreaded rocket-launchers, killed 611 Czechoslovak soldiers on that day.

Mountaineous terrain, awful weather and a well dug in enemy, all these factors radically changed the operation. What was planned as a swift penetration of German lines, immediatelly turned into a position warfare. The total Czechoslovak loss toll for the whole Carpatho-Dukla Operation was 935 dead, 4518 wounded and 756 missing...
