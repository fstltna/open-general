3rd Ypres Prelude - Messines Ridge - June 7th, 1917

Both British Prime Minister Lloyd George and French Chief of the General Staff General Foch oppose an Entente campaign in Flanders after the failed Nivelle Offensive and mutinies in the French Army. The British commander Douglas Haigh however got permission to launch an offensive. 

At 3:10 a.m. 19 mines - placed by British tunellers - were detonated under our positions on Messines Ridge. The explosions could be heard hundreds of kilometers away. British troops have gone over the top and are  attacking the ridge. You are in charge of the Eingreif Divisions and your task is to counterattack and regain our positions.
