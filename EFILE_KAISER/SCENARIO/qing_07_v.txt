Seymour's relief force never reached Peking. The foreign sailors fought bravely, but they weren't a serious menace for the Qing elite troops. The Westerner survivors retreated to Tientsin, where they hoped that they'd receive reinforcements very soon. We regrouped and chased them, but not before my father in law assured me that, after expelling the foreign army from China, we'd send our troops against the Yihetuan.
