March, 45 B.C. * Munda

....After Caesar had defeated the Pompeians almost everywhere, only some hispanic provinces remained to the pompeian Legions....
....The Pompeians were lead by Caesars former comrade-in-arms T. Labienus and the sons of the Pompey....
....By the favorable circumstances in the rich Hispania, and a ruthless taxation, the Pompeians could rapidly put up a big army again....

....Both armies met in the plain of Munda....
....Caesar saw, that at his right wing, the Legio X was involved in heavy fights, and personally took over the command there....

-"Normally I fight for the victory, here at Munda, however, for my life... "-
