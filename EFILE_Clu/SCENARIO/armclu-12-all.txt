After some initial successes, the German divisions were driven back to their starting points with heavy losses. According to the 9th Army's situation report of March 27th, five commanding officers, 68 officers and 1,219 enlisted men had been lost due to strong minefields, heavy mortar fire, antitank fire, accompanying artillery fire, well constructed strong points, and the lack of ground cover.
In fact the Germans had given the Soviets far too much time to consolidate their positions in a now well established bridgehead.
