   Siege of Odessa Part II 20 September 1941
___________________________________________

   Due to the heavy resistance of the 9th Independent Army (initially) and the rapidly formed Separate Coastal Army, which was formed from the Coastal Group of the 9th Army[1]), and the Black Sea Fleet forces in Odessa, it took the Romanian army 73 days of siege and four attempts to take the city, during which they suffered 93,000 casualties.

On 14 October 1941, most of the remaining forces of the Red Army were evacuated.
