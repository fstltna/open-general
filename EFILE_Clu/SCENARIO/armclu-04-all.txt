The attack broke down in the forests of Kunersdorf - the place where Fredrick the Great was defeated by the Russians in 1759. The next day the breakthrough attempt was renewed with the help of Colonel Rudel's Stukas and eventually succeeded. By february 4th the breakthrough had been completed. The entire division with all equipment and several thousand refugees arrived savely at the German bridgehead east of the Oder river. 
Colonel General Katukov described the fighting at Kunersdorf as the fiercest encountered during the entire Vistula-Oder Operation. The Soviets lost 73 tanks, 27 At-guns, 24 vehicles and many soldiers.
