The French were forced to retreat to Arras, in the north, in order to avoid the complete destruction of their army. 
The taking of Amiens improved the safety and the stability of the Prussian positions at the northern battlefront. However, the French units that could survive, with the help of new volunteers, kept on fighting against the Prussians until the last days of the war.

Meanwhile, in the German states the victories against France had started an unstoppable wave of patriotic fervour, which caused that Bavaria, Baden, Württemberg and Hesse joined the North German Confederation during November. The German unification was almost complete.
