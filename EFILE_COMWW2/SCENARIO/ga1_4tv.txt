Historcial Note: The invasion of Sicily was the logical continuation of the North African campaign, since capturing the island meant regaining almost complete control over the Mediterranean. The Allies also had a secondary goal: force Germany to pull land and air forces away from the Eastern front in order to defend it's Southern side, thereby easing the pressure on Russia. On July 4th and 5th, while enroute to Sicily, the Canadian assault convoy was attacked by U-boats off the coast of Algeria, losing 3 supply ships and over 500 vehicles, but none the less, Operation Husky was the campaign in which the Canadians started to come together as an effective fighting force...
