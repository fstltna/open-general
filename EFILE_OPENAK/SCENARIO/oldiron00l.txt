Failure! French batteries and ground troops took a heavy toll on the invasion force. Whatever is left of it, is brought back on board the ships. Your services are no longer required.


Historic events:

French batteries and ships of our invasion fleet exchanged fire throughout November 8-9 and French troops defended the area surrounding Oran stubbornly. But heavy fire from HMS Rodney brought about Oran's surrender on 9 November.

In Morocco a coup d'état of pro-Allied General Antoine Béthouart failed, but French troops surrendered on November 10th after heavy fighting on the beaches and a Naval battle at Cassablanca.

French opposition at Algiers was extremely light and the city surrendered at 18:00 on November8th - the day of the landings.
